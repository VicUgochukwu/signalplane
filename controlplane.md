# Signal Plane

**Competitive intelligence that ships every Monday.**

---

## The Problem

Every B2B GTM team tracks competitors. Almost none of them do it well.

The typical setup: someone sets a Google Alert, maybe checks a competitor's blog once a month, a rep drops something they heard on a call into Slack, and once a quarter someone pulls together a "competitive deck" that's outdated before the ink dries.

The result is decisions made on vibes, not evidence. Positioning that reflects last quarter's reality. Battlecards built from six-month-old screenshots. Sales teams walking into calls without knowing what their competitor shipped last Tuesday.

This isn't a people problem. It's an infrastructure problem. Competitive intelligence in most organizations isn't a system — it's a series of ad hoc rituals performed by overstretched PMMs who know they're falling behind but don't have a better option.

---

## What Signal Plane Does

Signal Plane is competitive intelligence infrastructure for B2B SaaS go-to-market teams.

Every week, automated monitors scan 10+ public signal types across your competitors — messaging changes, pricing edits, job postings, product launches, patent filings, executive moves, partnership announcements, review sentiment shifts, and more.

Those signals are scored on severity, recency, confidence, and source quality. Per-category caps prevent noise. Only the top 25 signals make the cut.

Every Monday, a structured decision packet ships to Slack, Notion, or email. Not a newsletter. Not a digest. A decision packet — executive summary, competitor deep-dives with source links, market predictions with confidence scores, impact scores rated on a 1–10 scale (Critical / High / Moderate / Low), and specific action items mapped to your GTM strategy.

Every claim traces to a source. Every score has a methodology. Every prediction gets tracked against outcomes.

---

## How It Works

**1. Collect**
Automated monitors scan public competitor signals weekly — messaging changes, narrative shifts, pricing edits, hiring patterns, and more. Teams can also bring internal data: Gong snippets, win/loss notes, support tickets, enrichment signals from tools like Clay.

**2. Score & Filter**
Every signal is scored 0–100 on severity, recency, confidence, and source quality. Per-category caps prevent any single signal type from dominating. Only the top 25 signals make the cut each week.

**3. Deliver**
Each Monday, a structured decision packet ships to Slack, Notion, or email — executive summary, key shifts, market predictions, competitive positioning leaderboard, and action items mapped to owners.

---

## What Ships in Every Packet

- **Executive Summary** — The 3–5 most important competitive shifts from the past week, each with an impact score (1–10) and confidence percentage.
- **Competitor Deep-Dives** — Detailed analysis organized by competitor: what changed, why it matters for your positioning, recommended response actions. Every insight links to the source material.
- **Market Predictions** — Forward-looking hypotheses with confidence percentages and supporting evidence. Tracked over time so accuracy improves as the system learns your market.
- **Market Winners** — Who's gaining and losing momentum based on signal volume, hiring patterns, and product velocity. A weekly leaderboard of competitive positioning.
- **Action Items** — Specific, prioritized actions mapped to your GTM strategy. Not generic suggestions — tailored recommendations based on that week's intelligence.

## GTM Artifacts

Beyond weekly packets, Signal Plane generates and maintains three living GTM documents that update automatically with every new packet:

- **Battlecards** — Side-by-side competitor comparisons pulled from real signals. Pricing changes, new features, positioning shifts — all current, all sourced.
- **Objection Handling Library** — The most common objections buyers raise about competitors, paired with evidence-backed responses your reps can use today.
- **Buyer Language Swipe File** — Actual phrases and terminology your buyers use when evaluating solutions. Built for sales scripts, landing pages, and ad copy.

These stay current. Every time a new intel packet drops, your artifacts update with the latest signals. No more stale battlecards from six months ago.

## What We're Not

- **Not a content aggregator.** Klue and Crayon collect content and require manual curation. Signal Plane scores automatically, enforces caps, maps to owners, tracks predictions against outcomes, and delivers structured packets.
- **Not an enrichment tool.** Clay enriches accounts and contacts (who to call). Signal Plane monitors competitor actions (what to say). They're complementary.
- **Not a news feed.** Signal Plane doesn't deliver a stream of links to scroll past. It delivers a structured decision artifact, once a week, with everything scored, ranked, and mapped to actions.

---

## How We Think About AI

LLM agents handle signal analysis and synthesis — identifying what changed, why it matters, and what to do about it. Scoring, category caps, and decision routing are deterministic: fixed rules, not AI guesswork. The AI does the thinking work; the system enforces the rigor.

Every claim is a hypothesis. If it can't be traced to a source, it doesn't go in the packet.
